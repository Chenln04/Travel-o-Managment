# 🛠️ Change Control & Governance: Travel-o

To ensure the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) remained within scope, budget, and schedule, we implemented a formal **Request for Change (RFC)** procedure.

## 1. The RFC Framework
Every proposed change underwent a structured evaluation using a standard form that captured:
* **Change ID & Source:** Identification of the requester and role.
* **Justification:** Detailed reasoning for the modification.
* **Impact Analysis:** Assessment of time, cost, and resource implications.
* **Technical Review:** Viability check against API constraints and AI compute limits.
* **Final Decision:** Approval or rejection by the Product Owner.

## 2. Change Management Procedure
We followed an 8-step lifecycle for every modification:
1. **Formal Request:** Submission of the RFC form.
2. **Initial Evaluation:** Coherence and data completeness check.
3. **Impact Analysis:** Technical assessment of risks and compatibility.
4. **Team Decision Meeting:** Collaborative debate on project convenience.
5. **Approval/Rejection:** Official sign-off by the Product Owner.
6. **Documentation Update:** Adjusting the WBS, Gantt, and technical docs.
7. **Implementation Monitoring:** Tracking the change during execution.
8. **Closure:** Verifying the final implementation and impact.
