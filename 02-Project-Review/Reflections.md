# 🧠 Project Coordination & Leadership Reflections

## Resource Allocation & Team Management
As the Project Coordinator for a team of 4, my primary focus was **maximizing efficiency through strength-based task assignment**. Instead of a random distribution, I allocated responsibilities based on individual skill sets:
* **Research & Data:** Assigned to team members with strong analytical skills.
* **Design & Documentation:** Led by those with high visual and communication proficiency.
* **Technical Writing:** Supervised to ensure a professional and cohesive final report.

## Crisis Management & Problem Solving
Throughout the academic cycle, we faced challenges regarding task completion and technical inconsistencies. My approach to leadership followed a clear **3-step framework**:
1. **Root Cause Identification:** Detecting bottlenecks or errors in real-time.
2. **Collaborative Decision Making:** Presenting the issue to the team and facilitating a brainstorm for solutions.
3. **Redistribution of Ownership:** Assigning clear responsibilities for the corrective actions to ensure the project stayed on track.

## Communication Strategy
In the absence of formal PM software at the time, I established a **Weekly Sync Meeting**. This served as our "Status Report" to:
* Track progress across different project modules.
* Identify early-stage difficulties.
* Ensure alignment between the UX design and the business logic.

## Lessons Learned & Future Growth
Reflecting on this simulation, I recognize that **visual transparency** is key to team performance. If I were to lead this project today, my first action would be implementing a **Visual Workflow (e.g., Trello or GitHub Projects)**. 

Moving forward, I am committed to integrating professional PM tools to transition from "informal coordination" to "data-driven project management," which I am currently mastering through my Google Project Management certification.
